Penguins are a group of aquatic flightless birds from the family Spheniscidae of the order Sphenisciformes. They live almost exclusively in the Southern Hemisphere: only one species, the Galápagos penguin, is found north of the Equator Penguins are stocky with short, strong legs. Prehistoric penguins stood 6 feet (1.8 meters) tall. Today the largest penguin is the emperor penguin, which is about 45 inches (115 centimeters) tall and weighs about 55–90 pounds (25–40 kilograms). One of the smallest penguins is the blue, or fairy, penguin. It is about 14 inches (35 centimeters) in height and about 2 pounds (1 kilogram) in weight.
